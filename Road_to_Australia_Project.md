# Road to Australia - Project System

Source of truth: `Road_to_Australia_Master_Plan.pdf`

Last structured: 24 June 2026

## Executive Summary

The project goal is long-term travel from the UK, departing on 15 September 2026, travelling slowly through Europe, the Balkans, Turkey and Southeast Asia, and eventually reaching Australia around February 2027. The plan combines travel, personal growth, TEFL-backed online income, content creation, poetry and building a sustainable lifestyle abroad.

## Assumptions And Recommendations

These are not stated as confirmed facts in the document. They are working assumptions or recommendations based on the plan.

- Currency is treated as GBP because the plan starts from the UK and the PDF budget amounts use a pound-style currency symbol.
- The 15 September 2026 departure should be treated as fixed for planning unless deliberately changed.
- The GBP 8,000 target should be treated as the minimum spendable travel fund, with emergency money held separately.
- Sydney is a starting point for Australia, not necessarily the final or only base.
- Online teaching should be set up before departure, not after arrival abroad.
- Content systems should be lightweight enough to survive travel days, hostel Wi-Fi and low-energy periods.
- The route should remain flexible after Istanbul because weather, visa rules, flight prices and paid work opportunities may shift the best path.

## Contradictions Or Tensions

- The route is ambitious for the goal of preserving capital. The plan can work, but only if Europe is travelled slowly and cheaply, with volunteering or low-cost stays used early.
- The GBP 8,000 target is described as spendable, while the plan also says emergency reserves are ideal. These should be separated into two numbers.
- Content creation, TEFL setup, admin and packing are all competing for the same June-August preparation window. The plan needs strict prioritisation.
- Carry-on travel is compatible with a 40L backpack plus daypack, but only if gear purchases are restrained and the capsule wardrobe stays disciplined.
- Sydney is an expensive first Australia base. It may still be good for work, but it increases early cash-flow risk after arrival.

## Risks

- Running down preparation money before departure.
- Overbuying gear.
- Leaving TEFL completion or teaching profile setup too late.
- Trying to move too fast through expensive European cities.
- Arriving in Australia without enough landing money.
- Treating content creation as an extra burden rather than a lightweight habit.
- Depending on online teaching before proving platform access and demand.
- Missing visa, insurance or passport details.

## Opportunities

- Budapest volunteering can reduce costs and create strong content.
- Chiang Mai could become a low-cost reset point for routine, teaching and writing.
- London to Istanbul is a clear first content series with a natural story arc.
- Poetry and reflective writing can make the project more distinctive than standard travel content.
- TEFL creates a practical bridge between travel and income.
- Slow travel can make the budget last much longer than a fast itinerary.

- Countdown and travel preparation progress.

- Editable route, city/country notes, documents, visas, packing and departure checklist.
- Budget income and expense entries, phase allocations, weekly/monthly summaries, spending charts and milestones.
- Budget pace calculations for safe daily/weekly spending and overspending warnings.
- Pre-departure, travel fund and emergency fund tracking.
- TEFL modules, hours remaining, required daily pace, study log, streaks and notes.
- TikTok, Instagram, YouTube and Substack content pipeline.
- Poetry, writing, daily check-ins, habits, journal and weekly review systems.
- JSON export/import for backups.
- Local browser storage, with migration from the previous planner data format.
